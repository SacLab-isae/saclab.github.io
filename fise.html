FISE Program

First Year Integrated Studies

The FISE program is designed to provide students with a solid foundation in aerospace engineering and related disciplines. During the first year, students will undertake a range of subjects including mathematics, physics, computer science, and introductory aerospace engineering courses. Students will also have the opportunity to participate in hands-on projects and team-based learning experiences, preparing them for more advanced studies in the subsequent years.

FISE 1A

First Year

TC

Core courses covering foundational topics in mathematics, physics, and computer science.

Electives

Students can choose electives based on their interests. One of the key electives is:

  • Maths & Espace: Advanced mathematics courses with a focus on space applications.

FISE 2A

Second Year

Meca Genes (CC)

Core courses in mechanics and general engineering principles.

Space Mechanics

Advanced courses in the mechanics of space systems and structures.

Space Systems

Courses focusing on the design and analysis of space systems, including satellites and spacecraft.

S4

Specialized courses in the fourth semester, including:

  • Human Spaceflight: Study of the challenges and systems involved in human space missions.

FISE 3A

Third Year

COS

Core courses and specialized electives in the third year.

  • CC: Core courses covering advanced topics in aerospace engineering.
  • PIE: Project-based courses and internships providing practical experience.

Transport

Courses focusing on the transportation systems and logistics of space missions.

SO

Specialized options in the final semester, allowing students to tailor their education to specific interests and career goals.
